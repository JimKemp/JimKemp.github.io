Summer internship in the lab developing Teradyne's autonomous pallet jack. My primary responsibility was building electronic measurement devices to measure the dimensions and position of pallets in Teradyne's testing lab. These devices provided "ground truth" data to compare the robot's estimation of pallet shape and position against, allowing its machine learning models to be improved. As a final project, I also built a small device that measures the slope of a warehouse floor, to allow estimates of pallet positions to be made even more precise.
I learned a tremendous amount from everyone in the lab, both about the robot's software and how a large-scale research and development project is run. Working at Teradyne also helped me improve as an engineer, because the lab had a culture of extreme precision. If the robot's forks miss their intended target position by even a centimeter, the pallet will be pushed along the floor rather than picked up. In the case of high-bay warehouses, this unintentional push could collapse a shelving unit and cause huge damage.
My day-to-day routine at Teradyne was split between the electrical/software work of designing and fabricating measurement/testing rigs and assisting the engineers in operating the warehouse analog used to train the rover's AI. In this way, I had a high volume of practice in circuit design and python alongside the opportunity to learn more about advanced machine learning algorithms.
