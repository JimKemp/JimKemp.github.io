Eagle Scout Project

Trop 89 Medfield

Skills Used
Mech: Woodworking
Teamwork: Technical Writing Feasibility Studies and Logistics Collabration with Nonprofits
Local News Article

To reach the rank of Eagle Scout with Medfield's Troop 89, I planned out and executed the contruction of three Little Free Libraries at group-living homes for people with disabilites. I'd previously done volunteer work wit Advocates, a nonprofit that works for adults with disabilities, and I was happy to make them the beneifciary of this project

The project had two phases - first, I organized a community book drive to stock the libraries, and then I began work on constructing the libraries themselves. The goal of an Eagle Scout project is to show leadership, and building your project yourself is discouraged. So I wrote a detailed instruction manual with diagrams explaining how to build a library, and had volunteers from the troop, all middle and high school students, built the libraries themselves. Looking back on the project, I'm proud of the plans and instructions I created, and my manual was subsequantly adopted by a community food pantry to build donation boxes as well as Advocates themselves for the contruction of more libraries. In fact, one of the libraries I built was later hit by a car and remained standing, intact. All three are still in use today.
